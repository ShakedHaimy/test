Conclusions

Despite the existence of numerous platforms attempting to address the initial problems, they have not gained widespread acceptance or captured a significant portion of the expansive cooking market. Thus, a comprehensive understanding of user needs is crucial to enhance Cookalachâ€™s prospects for success.

Analysis of user research indicates a demand for a culinary-focused social network, with users primarily seeking assistance in reducing cooking time and improving recipe search efficiency.

Users are accustomed to and actively engage with major social networks such as Facebook and Instagram. Their choices are influenced by mass opinions and recommendations. Therefore, the user experience should be familiar, comfortable, and reminiscent of their interactions on these social platforms.

A thorough examination of user preferences revealed both popular and diverse cuisines. It is imperative for the application to learn each user's personal taste, thereby presenting relevant recipes and cuisines. This personalized approach will optimize recipe selection and foster continued user engagement.

The registration process, a significant barrier for many competitors, will be streamlined and user-friendly, ensuring a swift and inviting experience.

Simplifying, clarifying, and enhancing the convenience of recipe search and filtering are critical factors contributing to the success of the application.

From a marketing perspective, an innovative strategy must be devised to make cooking sharing a popular trend. This will be achieved by facilitating sharing during every cooking experience, prominently featuring it on the home page, and employing an eye-catching design.
